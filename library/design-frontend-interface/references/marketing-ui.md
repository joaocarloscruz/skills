# Marketing Interfaces

Use these rules for landing pages, launches, campaigns, public websites, and experiences whose first impression and narrative matter.

## Build a truthful narrative

- Define the audience, promise, evidence, primary action, and objection the page must resolve.
- Make the opening section a clear thesis, not a generic headline beside an interchangeable illustration.
- Order sections as an argument: problem or opportunity, mechanism, proof, detail, objection handling, and action.
- Use concrete product language and real evidence. Do not invent customers, metrics, quotations, or awards.

## Create visual identity

- Derive color, typography, imagery, and graphic devices from the subject's world and existing brand.
- Choose one memorable compositional or interactive signature and keep surrounding sections disciplined.
- Treat typography and imagery as primary design material rather than decoration added after layout.
- Avoid automatic card grids, arbitrary gradients, oversized empty heroes, and repeated centered sections.

## Design conversion responsibly

- Keep one primary action visually dominant and name the result of taking it.
- Place proof near the claim it supports.
- Make price, limitations, requirements, and data collection understandable before commitment.
- Avoid deceptive urgency, disguised advertisements, preselected consent, or obstructive cancellation.

## Protect delivery quality

- Preserve reading order, meaningful headings, image alternatives, keyboard access, and reduced motion.
- Budget for font, media, script, and animation cost before choosing effects.
- Design narrow screens as deliberate compositions, not compressed desktop pages.
- Test real copy, slow loading, missing media, localization, and content-management variation.

## Quality test

Ask whether a visitor can explain what is offered, why it matters, why to trust it, and what the primary action will do after a brief scan.
